1. Compare spacing with padding Padding increases internal spacing inside items, while margin creates external spacing that affects surrounding layout. Padding does not separate items; margin does. ⸻ 2. Why is gap better than margin in flex layouts? Gap exists to provide consistent spacing between flex items without affecting container edges or requiring item-level spacing logic. It centralizes layout spacing control in the container. ⸻ 3. Why does gap exist at all? Because spacing between items is a layout concern, not an item concern. Gap prevents margin collisions, simplifies layouts, and improves maintainability.

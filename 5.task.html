Why does padding disallow negative values? Padding represents internal space inside an element. Negative padding would mean negative internal space, which is physically impossible in the box model. Therefore, browsers treat negative padding as invalid and ignore it. Answer 2: What are practical use cases for negative margins? Negative margins are useful when: • Creating overlapping layouts • Pulling elements closer together • Designing cards that overlap images • Adjusting alignment without changing HTML structure Negative margins affect external spacing, which is why they are allowed.

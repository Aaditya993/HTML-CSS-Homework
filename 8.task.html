Before flex, elements stacked vertically and relied on margins for spacing and alignment. Horizontal layouts required hacks like floats or inline-block. After applying flex, items automatically align in a row, spacing becomes predictable, and layout control moves to the parent container. Flex solves layout problems such as horizontal alignment, equal spacing, vertical centering, and responsiveness without relying on fragile margin tricks.
